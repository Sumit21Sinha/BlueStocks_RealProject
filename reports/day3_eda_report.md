## Key Findings

### 1. SIP inflows are growing consistently

Monthly SIP inflows increased from approximately ₹11,500 crore in 2022 to more than ₹31,000 crore by the end of the analysis period.

### 2. Mutual fund adoption is accelerating

Industry folios increased from around 13 crore to over 26 crore, indicating strong growth in investor participation.

### 3. T30 cities dominate investments

Approximately 66.3% of investors belong to T30 cities, while 33.7% belong to B30 cities.

### 4. Banking sector has the highest allocation

Banking accounts for nearly 19.2% of portfolio holdings, making it the largest sector exposure.

### 5. IT remains a major portfolio component

IT contributes approximately 13.4% of total portfolio allocation.

### 6. Pharma sector is strongly represented

Pharma contributes nearly 12% of overall portfolio allocation.

### 7. Most funds exhibit positive Sharpe ratios

The majority of schemes provide positive risk-adjusted returns.

### 8. A few funds significantly outperform peers

Several schemes demonstrate exceptionally high Sharpe ratios above 5.

### 9. Fund correlations remain relatively low

Most funds show weak correlations, indicating diversification benefits.

### 10. Overall industry outlook remains positive

Growth in SIP inflows, folios, and investor participation suggests a healthy and expanding mutual fund ecosystem.

---

## Conclusion

The analysis indicates sustained growth in the Indian mutual fund industry driven by increasing SIP participation, expanding investor base, and diversified investment opportunities. Banking, IT, and Pharma continue to dominate portfolio allocations, while investor participation from both T30 and B30 regions continues to increase. The overall industry demonstrates healthy long-term growth characteristics.
